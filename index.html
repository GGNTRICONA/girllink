Where It All Began

We started as a small community initiative, working directly with teenage girls in local schools. Through counseling sessions and support groups, we witnessed firsthand the challenges these young women face - from gender-based violence to educational barriers.

Our early work showed us that many girls were reluctant to seek help due to stigma, fear, and lack of trust in traditional support systems. We realized that while we were making an impact, we were only reaching a fraction of those who needed help.

The Digital Innovation

Recognizing that many girls needed anonymous ways to seek help, we began developing a comprehensive web platform. This digital solution addresses the trust barriers we encountered in our community work while scaling our impact beyond geographical limitations.

GirlLink Uganda represents the evolution of our mission - combining the personal touch of community counseling with the accessibility and safety of digital innovation.

Our Digital Platform

🔐 Anonymous Reporting

Safe, secure channels for reporting GBV, harassment, and other violations while protecting user identity and ensuring appropriate response.

🎓 Education Support

Direct access to school fees assistance, scholastic materials, and menstrual hygiene resources through verified partner organizations.

🤝 Counseling Access

Connect with trained counselors and mental health professionals through secure, confidential communication channels.

📍 Resource Mapping

Interactive map showing nearby health centers, police stations, NGOs, and safe spaces for immediate assistance.

📚 Learning Hub

Access to life skills training, reproductive health education, digital literacy, and inspirational success stories.

🔄 Case Management

Comprehensive system for tracking support requests, ensuring follow-up, and measuring impact across all interventions.

Meet Our Team

Wandera Cynthia

Founder & Lead Researcher

Institution: Trinity College Nabbingo

Cynthia combines academic excellence with passionate advocacy for girls' rights. Her hands-on experience working with over 150 girls in Eastern Uganda revealed the critical need for anonymous, accessible support systems.

With expertise in Social Sciences and Information Technology, she designed GirlLink Uganda to bridge the gap between traditional community support and modern digital solutions.

Her research focuses on gender-based violence prevention, educational access, and technology-enabled empowerment strategies for vulnerable populations.
